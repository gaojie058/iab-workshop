Interpreting Agent Behavior

How do agents behave, why do they fail, and how do humans make sense of it?

NeurIPS 2026 Workshop December 6–12, 2026 Sydney, Australia

About

AI agents powered by large language models are rapidly being deployed across software engineering, scientific research, and daily workflows. Yet a fundamental gap persists: we lack systematic methods for understanding how these agents behave, why they fail, and how humans interpret and respond to their actions.

Current evaluation paradigms rely heavily on automated benchmarks (e.g., SWE-bench, HumanEval) that measure task completion but reveal little about the behavioral dynamics underlying agent performance. When agents encounter knowledge conflicts, ambiguous instructions, or cascading errors, their behavior often becomes opaque to both developers and end users. At the same time, agentic systems generate rich interaction data, including execution logs, tool-use traces, reasoning chains, and conversation histories, that remain largely underexploited for behavioral analysis.

The Interpreting Agent Behavior (IAB) workshop addresses this gap by investigating agent behaviors from two complementary perspectives:

  • Mechanistic analysis of agent internals: planning errors, coordination breakdowns in multi-agent systems, hallucination patterns, failure cascades, and behavior under knowledge conflicts.
  • Interactional analysis of human-agent dynamics: trust calibration, over-reliance patterns, verification strategies, mental model formation, and the cognitive processes humans use to make sense of agent actions.

By treating "interpretation" as both a technical diagnostic and a human cognitive process, we aim to establish a unified vocabulary and research agenda for building agents that are not only performant but also legible to their users.

Topics of Interest

  • Agent Behavior Under Uncertainty: How do agents handle ambiguous instructions, conflicting knowledge, or incomplete information?
  • Failure Mode Analysis: Systematic taxonomies of why agents fail, spanning planning errors, tool misuse, hallucination, and cascading failures in multi-agent settings.
  • Trust and Reliance Dynamics: How do humans calibrate trust in agent outputs? When does over-reliance emerge, and what verification strategies do people develop?
  • Mental Models of Agent Behavior: How do users form and update mental models of what agents can and cannot do?
  • Debugging and Auditing Agentic Systems: Methods, tools, and interfaces for diagnosing agent behavior, from log analysis to interactive debugging.
  • Multi-Agent Coordination and Breakdown: How do agents coordinate (or fail to coordinate) in multi-agent systems?
  • Evaluation Beyond Benchmarks: Human-centered evaluation methodologies that capture behavioral quality, not just task completion.

Call for Papers

We invite work that advances the understanding, interpretation, and evaluation of agent behavior, including but not limited to the topics listed above. Submissions may report new results, negative findings, benchmarks, position papers, system demos, or visionary perspectives.

  • Long papers: up to 9 pages (excluding references)
  • Short papers: up to 4 pages (excluding references)
  • Formatting: NeurIPS 2026 style files, single anonymous PDF
  • Appendix: supplementary material in the same PDF, no page limit
