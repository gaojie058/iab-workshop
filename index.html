Interpreting Agent Behavior

Understanding how agents behave, why they fail, and how humans make sense of it

NeurIPS 2026 Workshop · Vancouver, Canada · December 2026

About

AI agents powered by large language models are rapidly being deployed across software engineering, scientific research, and daily workflows. Yet a fundamental gap persists: we lack systematic methods for understanding how these agents behave, why they fail, and how humans interpret and respond to their actions.

Current evaluation paradigms rely heavily on automated benchmarks (e.g., SWE-bench, HumanEval) that measure task completion but reveal little about the behavioral dynamics underlying agent performance. When agents encounter knowledge conflicts, ambiguous instructions, or cascading errors, their behavior often becomes opaque. At the same time, agentic systems generate rich interaction data, including execution logs, tool-use traces, and conversation histories, that remain largely underexploited for behavioral analysis.

The Interpreting Agent Behavior (IAB) workshop addresses this gap by investigating agent behaviors from two complementary perspectives:

  • Mechanistic analysis of agent internals: planning errors, coordination breakdowns, hallucination patterns, failure cascades, and behavior under knowledge conflicts.
  • Interactional analysis of human-agent dynamics: trust calibration, over-reliance patterns, verification strategies, mental model formation, and cognitive processes humans use to make sense of agent actions.

The scope encompasses both single-agent systems and multi-agent systems (e.g., collaborative agent teams, orchestrated workflows, multi-agent debate). By treating "interpretation" as both a technical diagnostic and a human cognitive process, we aim to establish a unified vocabulary and research agenda for building agents that are not only performant but also legible to their users.

Call for Papers

We invite work that advances understanding of agent behavior from technical, human-centered, or interdisciplinary perspectives, including but not limited to:

  • Agent behavior under uncertainty, knowledge conflicts, and ambiguous instructions
  • Failure mode taxonomies for single-agent and multi-agent systems
  • Trust calibration, over-reliance, and verification strategies in human-agent collaboration
  • Mental models of agent behavior and their impact on collaboration quality
  • Methods, tools, and interfaces for debugging and auditing agentic systems
  • Evaluation methodologies beyond task-completion benchmarks
  • Leveraging interaction logs, traces, and conversation histories for behavioral analysis

Submissions may report new results, negative findings, benchmarks, system demos, or visionary perspectives.

Submission Guidelines
  • Long papers: up to 9 pages (excluding references)
  • Short papers: up to 4 pages (excluding references)
  • Format: NeurIPS 2026 style files, single anonymized PDF
  • Non-archival: authors are free to publish revised versions elsewhere
